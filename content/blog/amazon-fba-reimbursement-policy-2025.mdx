## January 2025: the shortened claim-window mindset

Although Amazon adjusts windows by claim type over time, seller consensus solidified early in 2025 around one cultural shift: procrastination carries a hard cost. Many brands historically audited reimbursements quarterly or annually, comfortable mining long lookback horizons when cash flow allowed.

Compressed administrative windows—for many dispute classes effectively on the order of **60 days once an issue is surfaced**—force weekly triage rituals. Waiting until “after Prime Day” risks permanently forfeiting reimbursement paths that depended on contemporaneous carton photos or carrier paperwork.

## Manufacturing-cost reimbursement realism

Beyond timing, Amazon’s emphasis on validating **seller-submitted manufacturing or replacement cost proofs** reshapes economics. Sellers who anchored claims on hypothetical retail valuations must migrate to invoices, ethically supportable BOM rollups where applicable, and landed-cost constructs that reconcile to actual payments.

Thin documentation incentivizes Amazon’s internal estimation methods—seller communities frequently cite **meaningful undervaluation** when Amazon substitutes its own calculators. Maintain immutable COGS files keyed to supplier PO IDs, Harmonized SKU mapping to ASINs, and rework logs capturing quality fallout that changes per-unit averages.

Operational nuance applies across categories differently: meltable groceries, textiles with subjective QC damage calls, aggregated meltable shipments, lithium-battery gated SKUs—all shift adjudication friction.**Documentations win disputes**, not spreadsheets of intent.
